\## Short Bio



Pavan Dev Singh Charak is an independent systems builder focused on developing infrastructure for \*\*verifiable automated decision systems\*\*.



His work explores the intersection of:



\- automated decision systems

\- cryptographic verification

\- governance infrastructure

\- transparency registries



He is currently building the \*\*Decision Integrity Protocol (DIP)\*\* — an open protocol designed to enable systems to produce \*\*cryptographically verifiable decision artifacts\*\* that can be independently validated.



The long-term goal of this work is to establish \*\*verifiable decision infrastructure\*\* for automated systems across artificial intelligence, finance, governance, and regulatory environments.



\## Vision



Modern automated systems increasingly make decisions affecting:



\- financial systems  

\- artificial intelligence models  

\- governance platforms  

\- regulatory compliance systems  



However, most systems produce \*\*no verifiable evidence describing how decisions were generated\*\*.



The Decision Integrity Protocol introduces a framework for producing:



\- verifiable decision artifacts  

\- cryptographic signatures  

\- append-only transparency registries  

\- independent verification systems  



This enables automated decisions to become \*\*verifiable digital records\*\*.

\## Current Work



The protocol ecosystem currently includes several repositories:



\- \*\*dip-spec\*\* — protocol specification  

\- \*\*dip-cli\*\* — signing and verification tooling  

\- \*\*dip-registry\*\* — append-only transparency registry  

\- \*\*dip-go-verifier\*\* — verification library  

\- \*\*dip-website\*\* — documentation and research  



These repositories form the \*\*initial reference implementation of the protocol\*\*.
