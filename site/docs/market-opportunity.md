\# Market Opportunity



\## The Rise of Automated Decision Systems



Modern digital infrastructure increasingly relies on automated decision systems.



Artificial intelligence models, financial automation platforms, compliance engines, and digital governance systems now make billions of decisions every day.



These decisions affect:



\- financial transactions

\- regulatory compliance

\- fraud detection

\- governance processes

\- risk management

\- AI system outcomes



Despite their importance, most automated decision systems do \*\*not produce verifiable evidence describing how decisions were generated\*\*.



This creates a growing infrastructure gap.



The Decision Integrity Protocol addresses this gap by enabling \*\*verifiable decision infrastructure\*\*.



\# Why This Problem Is Growing



Several global trends are accelerating demand for verifiable decision systems.



\## AI Expansion



Artificial intelligence systems are rapidly being deployed across industries.



Examples include:



\- financial risk models

\- recommendation systems

\- automated customer support

\- hiring systems

\- predictive analytics



As AI systems make more decisions, the need for \*\*accountability and verification\*\* increases.



\## Regulatory Pressure



Governments worldwide are introducing regulations that require organizations to demonstrate how automated systems produce decisions.



Examples include:



\- AI governance regulations

\- financial transparency requirements

\- compliance auditing frameworks



Organizations must increasingly prove the integrity of their automated systems.



\## Automation of Critical Infrastructure



Automated systems are expanding into critical infrastructure such as:



\- banking systems

\- supply chain management

\- autonomous transportation

\- digital public infrastructure



These systems require \*\*verifiable evidence of decision processes\*\*.



\# Market Categories Impacted

Decision artifacts provide verifiable evidence of AI decisions.

Regulatory technology platforms help organizations comply with complex regulations.

Autonomous systems such as vehicles and automated agents require verifiable logs of system decisions.



Decision artifacts provide tamper-evident evidence for these systems.
